# Target Users – MVP focus

This document defines the core user segments specifically for the MVP launch of GoShopMe. Only groups directly involved in the MVP experience are included.

## 1. Primary User — The Shopper (MVP Core)

### Profile
Fashion-interested consumers who want:
• Faster shopping
• Better outfit suggestions
• An assistant who finds items for them
• A simple checkout experience
• The ability to earn by sharing products with friends

### Key Characteristics
• Mostly women ages 18–55
• Active online shoppers
• Follow fashion creators on social media
• Want high-quality, stylish items without spending hours searching
• Often buy for work, travel, dating, events, and daily outfits

### Core Needs (MVP)
• Style quiz–based personalization
• Chat-based product discovery
• Upload image/video → get outfit reconstruction and similar items
• Price-sensitive alternatives (luxury vs. cheaper versions)
• Add to cart + conversational checkout
• Social shopping → share items with friends
• Earn commission when a friend signs up and buys

### Why They Use GoShopMe
• Saves time and effort
• Produces better outfit suggestions than browsing alone
• Matches or recreates outfits from Instagram/TikTok
• Helps them shop smarter and cheaper
• Offers financial incentives through shopper commissions

## 2. Internal Stylists (Not public; strictly for MVP content)

### Role
These are internal team members, not external creators.
They produce:
• Curated collections ("Creator Picks")
• Lookbooks
• Demo styling content

### Why They Exist
• Ensure high-quality outputs for early users
• Remove dependency on external creators during MVP
• Maintain a consistent brand aesthetic

## 3. Friends of Shoppers (MVP Social Growth Loop)

### Profile
Users who join after being invited by a shopper.

### Their Role in MVP
• Join group chats
• Receive shared items
• Complete purchases
• Unlock commissions for the original shopper

### Why They Matter
• They drive viral growth
• They convert better because recommendations come from someone they trust
• They help build a social, collaborative shopping experience

## 4. Demo-Only Merchants (Internal MVP Catalog)

### Profile
These are not real vendors, but internal/dummy merchants supplying product catalogs for:
• Search
• Recommendations
• Internet-wide fallback matching

### Why They Are Included
• To make the AI personal shopper functional before actual merchant onboarding
• To simulate a realistic shopping experience
• To avoid dependencies on third-party APIs during MVP

## Summary Table (Narrow & MVP-Focused)

| Segment | Included in MVP | Purpose |
